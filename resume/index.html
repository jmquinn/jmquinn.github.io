Summary

I started my career at Michigan State University after graduating from the same institution. My responsibilities included administering enterprise storage arrays and Linux servers within the central academic-focused IT department on campus. Largely self-taught and self-motivated, I familiarized myself with the various systems and grew to become an expert in both fields.

After a reorganization, I was promoted to manager of a newly created Virtualization, Storage and Systems team. My team was responsible for managing 14 VMWare clusters located in various datacenters across campus serving over 1300 virtual servers, managing 10 Enterprise-class storage arrays serving over 500 TB of data, a roughly 100 TB backup operation, and over 400 administered Windows and Linux servers that support systems such as our enterprise business applications: SAP, IBM Cognos, and Kuali Financial System.

I stepped down from my management position for personal reasons, which allowed me to work remotely for a period of time as part of the Infrastructure Design team. I have since resumed woroking on-site, and continue to spearhead and complete many of the projects I started as a manager, as well as taking on new roles to further the mission of the organization.

My many years of experience at Michigan State University, a state-funded, world renowned research and educational institution have equipped me with the knowledge and understanding of highly sophisticated and high-importance operations.
